<p>I read 20 books last year. All twenty books were deadtree format. Fourteen of them were fiction. Six were non-fiction. I continued to read to the girls almost every night. Podcasts still loom large in terms of time invested.</p>

<p>There’s a chart below showing books read by year since 2011.</p>

<h3>Fiction in 2018</h3>

<ul><li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Cuckoo%27s_Calling">The Cuckoo’s Calling</a> - J. K. Rowling as Robert Galbraith - Tropey brit-bloke detective novel. Fun. I’ll follow this series.</li>
<li><a href="https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/16071377-the-vorrh">The Vorrh</a> - Brian Catling - An endless, timeless African forest. Colonialism and ancient magic.</li>
<li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wuthering_Heights">Wuthering Heights</a> - Emily Brontë - An exploration of tyranny and love on the heath.</li>
<li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/All_the_Birds_in_the_Sky">All The Birds in the Sky</a> - Charlie Jane Anders - Witchcraft and science in the near-future.</li>
<li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_Wrinkle_in_Time">A Wrinkle In Time</a> - Madeleine L'Engle - The Many-Worlds interpretation of quantum mechanics for kids.</li>
<li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jonathan_Livingston_Seagull">Jonathan Livingston Seagull</a> - Richard Bach - Positive thinking and tenacity for birds.</li>
<li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Terra_Ignota#Seven_Surrenders">Seven Surrenders</a> - Ada Palmer - Providence in another God’s universe.</li>
<li><a href="https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/23847937-democracy">Democracy</a> - Papadatos, Kawa &amp; Di Donna - 490 BCE Athens as graphic novel.</li>
<li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Lies_of_Locke_Lamora">The Lies of Locke Lamora</a> - Scott Lynch - Gentlemen Bastards serve the Crooked Warden in medieval Venice, but on another planet.</li>
<li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Our_Man_in_Havana">Our Man in Havana</a> - Graham Greene - Spycraft and other self-fufilling prophecies.</li>
<li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Buried_Giant">Buried Giant</a> - Kazuo Ishiguro - A quest to remember in a time with a need to forget.</li>
<li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reamde">REAMDE</a> - Neal Stephenson - Gold farming and jihad.</li>
<li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2001:_A_Space_Odyssey_(novel)">2001: A Space Odyssey</a> - Arthur C. Clarke - “oh my God! — <em>it’s full of stars!</em>”</li>
<li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Terra_Ignota#The_Will_to_Battle">The Will To Battle</a> - Ada Palmer - A tract of time, wherein the Will to contend by Battle is sufficiently known.</li>
</ul><p>Read in that order.</p>

<h3>Top Three Fiction in 2018</h3>

<p><strong><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wuthering_Heights">Wuthering Heights</a> by Emily Brontë</strong></p>

<blockquote>
  <p>“I have dreamt in my life, dreams that have stayed with me ever after, and changed my ideas; they have gone through and through me, like wine through water, and altered the color of my mind. And this is one: I’m going to tell it - but take care not to smile at any part of it.”</p>
</blockquote>

<p>This book came <a href="https://redlibrarian.github.io/article/2018/12/20/books-in-review-2018.html">highly recommend by Sam</a>. I’m not sure what I was expecting, but it wasn’t an exploration of tyranny, love, and inter-generational trama on the heath.</p>

<p>From Sam:</p>

<blockquote>
  <p>“This is a wild ride: raw and violent and exceedingly modern. In Wuthering Heights there’s no hiding how terrible most of the people in it are. At the same time, the novel seems to be precisely about how conditions of violence, cruelty, racism, and intolerance reproduce themselves from generation to generation.”</p>
</blockquote>

<p>At the end I was hoping for a Nelly spin-off.</p>

<p><strong><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Terra_Ignota#Seven_Surrenders">Seven Surrenders</a> and  <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Terra_Ignota#The_Will_to_Battle">The Will to Battle</a> by Ada Palmer</strong></p>

<blockquote>
  <p>“If God made Man and Man made this, it is still a Self-portrait. And if, as some say, God made Man in His Image, and His Image then made this, it is a portrait’s portrait. And if Nature is the face of God, another Portrait, and Man is the spawn of Nature, it becomes a portrait’s portrait’s portrait. The Nature we see on Earth too is a microcosm, one might say a portrait of the Cosmos, and the Cosmos a portrait of the Laws of Nature, portraits spawning portraits like the spiral chambers of a nautilus repeating the face of God. Such a Creator seems desperate to show Himself to someone. And yet He hides Himself.”</p>
</blockquote>

<p>Part two and three of the Terra Ignota Series. Philosophical and political sci-fi of the best kind.</p>

<p>Is there a place for miracle in a scientific society?</p>

<p>Is an Imperfect Sovereign better than a mounting Will to Battle?</p>

<p>What exactly is it that holds society together?</p>

</ul><h3>Top Three Non-Fiction in 2018</h3>

<p><strong><a href="https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/71730.Nonviolent_Communication">Nonviolent Communication</a> by  Marshall B. Rosenberg</strong></p>

<blockquote>
  <p>“Nonviolent Communication holds that most conflicts arise from coercive or manipulative language that aims to induce fear, guilt, and shame. These "violent” modes of communication divert the attention of the participants away from clarifying their needs, their feelings, their perceptions, and their requests, thus perpetuating the conflict.“ 
  -<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nonviolent_Communication">Wikipedia Entry on NVC</a></p>
</blockquote>

<p><strong><a href="https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/31415654-becoming-leonardo">Becoming Leonardo</a> by  Mike Langford</strong></p>

<blockquote>
  <p>"What accounts for Leonardo is an act of self-discovery, and the tenacity to make it, over and over. […] Learning to read was incredibly difficult, writing “correctly” even more so. Everything seemed wrong to him, backward somehow, and he couldn’t figure out why. He felt so stupid. And then, somehow the idea was inserted into his confused little brain, “Do it your own way, even if it is different. You are not stupid! Find how it works for you.”</p>
</blockquote>

<p><strong><a href="https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/30753841-salt-fat-acid-heat">Salt Fat Acid Heat</a> by Samin Nosrat. Illustrated by Wendy MacNaughton.</strong></p>

<blockquote>
  <p>“Play to each element’s strengths: use Salt to enhance, Fat to carry, and Acid to balance flavor.”</p>
</blockquote>

<p>A cookbook for those who wish to cook without cookbooks.</p>

<h3>Podcasts in 2018</h3>
