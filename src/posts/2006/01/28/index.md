&gt;**It** is interesting to note that orthodox w[Hinduism](Hinduism) believes that the language of the w[Vedas](Vedas) is eternal and revealed in its wording and word order. Evidence for this belief is found in the w[Vedas](Vedas) itself, where in the w[Upanishads](Upanishads) they are described as the very "breath of God" (nihsvasitam brahma). 
&gt;
&gt;The w[Vedas](Vedas) are therefore considered **"[the language of reality](http://www.google.ca/search?q=%22the+language+of+reality%22)"**, so to speak, and are *unauthored, even by God*, the w[rishis](rishis) or seers ascribed to them being merely individuals gifted with a special insight into reality with the power of perceiving these eternal sounds. At the beginning of every cycle of creation, *God himself "remembers"* the order of the Vedic words and propagates them through the rishis.
&gt;
&gt;It may be interesting to note that w[Hinduism](Hinduism) encourages the Vedic mantras to be interpreted as liberally and as philosophically as possible.
