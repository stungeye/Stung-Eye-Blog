<p>Coding is hard because it’s two things:</p>

<ol><li>Expressing ideas in the rigid syntax &amp; grammar of a formal language.</li>
<li>Problem solving.</li>
</ol><p>While learning to code we often focus too much on the first. This is also true while teaching others to code.</p>

<p>This fall I’m going to highlight problem solving while teaching my intro programming courses. Below you will find some of the problem solving strategies I may adapt for my students.</p>

<h3>Solving Problems in Five Acts</h3>

<ol><li>Define the Problem</li>
<li>Let it Simmer</li>
<li>Plan a Solution</li>
<li>Carry out the Plan</li>
<li>Reflect</li>
</ol><p>via: <a href="https://uwaterloo.ca/centre-for-teaching-excellence/teaching-resources/teaching-tips/developing-assignments/cross-discipline-skills/teaching-problem-solving-skills">University of Waterloo Centre for Teaching Excellence</a></p>

<h3>FOWL Problem Solving</h3>

<p><strong>F</strong>igure out What You’re Being Asked<br><strong>O</strong>rganize the Presented Data<br><strong>W</strong>ork out the Problem<br><strong>L</strong>ook Over Your Answer</p>

<p>via: <a href="http://geekdad.com/2015/07/gpa-learn-4/">Geekdad</a></p>

<h3>George Pólya’s Problem Solving Techniques</h3>

<ol><li>Understand the Problem</li>
<li>Devise a Plan</li>
<li>Carry out the Plan</li>
<li>Look Back</li>
</ol><p>via: <a href="https://math.berkeley.edu/~gmelvin/polya.pdf">George Melvin - Berkeley University</a></p>

<h3>Feedback</h3>

<p><em>How do you solve problems?</em></p>

<p>On reviewing this post <a href="https://twitter.com/redlibrarian">Sam</a> <a href="https://twitter.com/stungeye/status/619890361792614400">suggested</a> emphasising hypothesis and testing when teaching these strategies. I agree that formulating a hypothesis during planning makes room for false starts, while testing adds rigure to the self-assesment of the “Look Back”/Reflect phases. The wonderful part about making these two steps explicit is that we now have something akin to <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scientific_method">the scientific method</a>:</p>

<ol><li>Statement of the problem.</li>
<li>Hypotheses as to the cause of the problem.</li>
<li>Experiments designed to test each hypothesis.</li>
<li>Predicted results of the experiments.</li>
<li>Observed results of the experiments</li>
<li>Conclusions from the results of the experiments.</li>
</ol><p><strong>Language geek note:</strong> The word solve comes from the Latin <a href="http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Perseus:text:1999.04.0059:entry=solvo">solvo</a>, <em>to loose an object bound, to release, set free, disengage, dissolve, take apart.</em></p>
