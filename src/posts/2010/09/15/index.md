[How Piracy Works](http://notch.tumblr.com/post/1121596044/how-piracy-works)

<p>A post on turning pirates into customers by the creator of <a href="http://www.minecraft.net/">Minecraft</a>.</p>

<p><em>&#8220;Large parts of the culture these days exists in a world where copies are free. Copying a physical book costs money, but copying a digital movie is free. [&#8230;] No resources are lost, nobody loses any money, and more people are having fun. [&#8230;]</em></p>

<p><em>All of society and economics is based on an old outdated model where giving something to someone would rid the original owner of their copy. [&#8230;] For example, for every wheel in the market, someone had to make that wheel. With digital copies, you only need to make the wheel once. [&#8230;]</em></p>

<p><em>If someone pirates <a href="http://www.minecraft.net/">Minecraft</a> instead of buying it, it means I’ve lost some &#8216;potential&#8217; revenue. Not actual revenue, as I can never go into debt by people pirating the game too much.&#8221;</em></p>
