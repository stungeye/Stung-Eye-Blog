<p><a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mwDKMiAfxFE">This video</a> shows two AI players battling it out for <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tron_%28arcade_game%29">Tron</a> supremacy as part of the <a href="http://csclub.uwaterloo.ca/contest/index.php">Google AI Challenge</a>.</p>

<p>If you&#8217;ve ever wanted to learn about game-AI programming you might want to look into this challenge. Bots can be written in C++, Java, Perl, Python, Ruby, Haskell, C#, Javascript, Go, Scheme, Lua and Clojure.</p>

<p>If you don&#8217;t know where to begin, you can try your hand at learning game-AI by playing <a href="http://github.com/ryanb/ruby-warrior">Ruby Warrior</a>, a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roguelike">roguelike</a> that you play by implementing <a href="http://www.cubiclemuses.com/cm/articles/2009/03/15/reversion-of-control-in-ruby-warrior/">a Ruby class containing your player&#8217;s control logic</a>.</p>
