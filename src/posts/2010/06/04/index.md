<p>On Thursday the Canadian government introduced the Copyright Modernization Act (or <a href="http://www2.parl.gc.ca/HousePublications/Publication.aspx?Docid=4580265&amp;file=4">Bill C-32</a>).</p>

<p>The CBC does a good job of outlining <a href="http://www.cbc.ca/technology/story/2010/06/02/copyright-bill-clement-montreal.html">the proposed changes to Canadian copyright law</a>.</p>

<p>The bill is an attempt to strike a fair balance between:</p>

<ol>
<li>Our ability to monetize creativity. <em>-vs-</em> Our ability to re-purpose culture. </li>
<li>A creator’s right to control their works. <em>-vs-</em> A consumer’s rights to experience purchased media flexibly and in perpetuity.</li>
</ol>
<p>There are things to like in this bill. The <a href="http://www2.parl.gc.ca/HousePublications/Redirector.aspx?RefererUrl=%2fHousePublications%2fPublication.aspx%3fDocId%3d4580265%26Language%3de%26Mode%3d1&amp;File=48#8">format-shifting</a>, <a href="http://www2.parl.gc.ca/HousePublications/Redirector.aspx?RefererUrl=%2fHousePublications%2fPublication.aspx%3fDocId%3d4580265%26Language%3de%26Mode%3d1&amp;File=51#9">time-shifting</a> and <a href="http://www2.parl.gc.ca/HousePublications/Redirector.aspx?RefererUrl=%2fHousePublications%2fPublication.aspx%3fDocId%3d4580265%26Language%3de%26Mode%3d1&amp;File=54#10">backup provisions</a> are long-overdue, as is the expansion of fair-dealings<sup>1</sup>. The <a href="http://www2.parl.gc.ca/HousePublications/Publication.aspx?DocId=4580265&amp;Language=e&amp;Mode=1&amp;File=45#7">non-commercial “mash-up/youtube” provisions</a> are indeed progressive.</p>

<p><em>However</em>, any and all use-rights provided by the bill are <strong>revoked</strong> if the work in questions is protected by a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_rights_management">digital lock</a>. This immediately makes backing up DVDs illegal. It also makes viewing DVDs using the Linux operating-system illegal<sup>2</sup>. Copying a quote from a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_rights_management">DRM</a>-locked e-book for a book report or a news story would be illegal too.</p>

<p>The supremacy of digital-locks promoted by this bill must not be allowed to pass into law. If you value free-speech, your ability to re-purpose culture, and your right to use your purchased media as you see fit, I ask that you write your Member of Parliament to express your displeasure over the DRM provisions in Bill C32. (If anything, a bill that includes DRM provisions should mandate explicit labeling of all digitally-locked media.)</p>

<p>You can <a href="http://openparliament.ca/">find your MP</a> by searching for your postal code on <a href="http://openparliament.ca/">openparliament.ca</a>.</p>
