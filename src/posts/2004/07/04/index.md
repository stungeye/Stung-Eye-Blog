The much hyped book, <a href="http://www.randomhouse.com/bantamdell/theruleoffour/index.html" target="_blank">The Rule of Four</a>,  centres on a 15th-century manuscript, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hypnerotomachia_Poliphili" target="_blank"><em>The Hypnerotomachia Poliphili</em></a>. Although the book is a work of fiction, the manuscript truly exists.

"The action of the Hypnerotomachia Poliphili takes place in a dream. The books opens on the hero, Poliphilo, who has spent a restless night because his beloved, Polia, has shunned him. At the break of day, he finally falls into a deep slumber and his 'Hypnerotomachia,' or, as it can be roughly translated, 'struggle for love in a dream,' begins."

The above quote was taken from <a href="http://mitpress.mit.edu/e-books/HP/hyptext0.htm" target="_blank">this e-book</a>, which explores the mysterious manuscript.

<a href="http://mitpress.mit.edu/e-books/HP/hyp000.htm" target="_blank">A scanned copy of the <em>HP</em></a> is also available.
