***<BR>
I came across a <a href="http://www.theeducatedmachine.com/carole/archives/2004/01/02/monkey_tail_plant.html" target="_blank">monkey tail plant</a> while wandering within the woods. Soon after, I was approached by two men with tape recorders strapped to their backs. <a href="http://www.traffic-island.co.uk/" target="_blank">They wanted the music that was playing in my walkman</a>. I explained my search to them; the quest for <a href="http://www11.ocn.ne.jp/%7Eorigami/index.htm" target="_blank">contemporary origami</a>. Hopefully the origami <a href="http://www.undergroundpress.org/" target="_blank">zine</a> I picked up in town would provide the necessary clues.<BR>
I continued <a href="http://www.theeducatedmachine.com/neueshue/archives/001253.html" target="_blank">walking</a>. My mind began to drift back to my warm room and the meditative nature of <a href="http://www.craftster.org/" target="_blank">crafts</a>.<BR>
Lost in thought, an idea arose: "<a href="http://dominionpaper.ca/features/2004/02/03/what_if_we.html" target="_blank">What if we gave it all away?</a>"<BR>
More ideas flooded the pathways: <a href="http://www.discover.com/issues/nov-03/departments/reviews/?page=3" target="_blank">The visual nature of language</a>; <a href="http://www.superliminal.com/fractals/bbrot/bbrot.htm" target="_blank">Is the Mandelbrot set fractal simply an image of the Buddha sitting in meditation</a> (<a href="http://www.complexification.net/gallery/machines/buddahbrot/" target="_blank">more buddhabrot</a>); <a href="http://www.newyorker.com/archive/content/?011126fr_archive02" target="_blank">what's on the other side of the...</a>
