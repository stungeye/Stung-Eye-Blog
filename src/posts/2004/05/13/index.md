## Digital Memory

Whereas the last post dealt with books, this post will highlight what is keeping my mind busy online.

<a href="http://www.nchicha.com/cupofchicha/" target="_blank">Natalie Chicha</a>'s new blog, <a href="http://www.nchicha.com/other/" target="_blank">Another</a>, provides "an online space for [her] thoughts on depression and literature." Two interesting posts:
<ul><li><a href="http://www.nchicha.com/other/archives/002774.html#002774" target="_blank">"Does one have to be insane to be creative?"</a></li>
<li><a href="http://www.nchicha.com/other/archives/002757.html#002757" target="_blank">I Married Language (And Now She Doesn't Want to Fuck Me)</a></li></ul>
The editorial board of <a href="http://www.cognitivesciencesociety.org/" target="_blank">Cognitive Science</a> has made available <a href="http://cognitrn.psych.indiana.edu/rgoldsto/cogsci/classics.html" target="_blank">10 classics from their archives</a>.

<a href="http://www.themodernword.com/eco/eco_writings.html" target="_blank">Unheard-of Curiosities</a> is a collection of writings by Umberto Eco. <a href="http://www.themodernword.com/eco/eco_guardian94.html" target="_blank">A Rose by Any Other Name</a>, deals with the problems of literary translation.

<a href="http://www.246.dk/38tricks.html" target="_blank">Thirty-eight dishonest tricks</a> which are commonly used in argument, with the methods of overcoming them. A look at straight and crooked thinking by Robert H. Thouless.

A musical ebook series: <a href="http://www.musique.umontreal.ca/personnel/Belkin/bk/index.html" target="_blank">Form</a>, <a href="http://www.musique.umontreal.ca/personnel/Belkin/bk.C/index.html" target="_blank">Counterpoint</a>, <a href="http://www.musique.umontreal.ca/personnel/Belkin/bk.H/index.html" target="_blank">Harmony</a> and <a href="http://www.musique.umontreal.ca/personnel/Belkin/bk.o/" target="_blank">Artistic Orchestration</a>.

Some chapters from the 2002 revision of <a href="http://www.slofi.com/About_the_book.htm" target="_blank">The Social Life of Information</a> are <a href="http://www.slofi.com/toc.htm" target="_blank">available online</a>.

<a href="http://www.paulgraham.com/articles.html" target="_blank">Paul Graham's essays</a> touch on everything from <a href="http://www.paulgraham.com/hp.html" target="_blank">hackers and painters</a> to <a href="http://www.paulgraham.com/taste.html" target="_blank">personal taste</a>.

Finally, <a href="http://www.jengajam.com/r/shirt-folding" target="_blank">the coolest way to fold a t-shirt</a>, ever. [Quicktime]

I'm addicted to information. Books have always been my favourite knowledge narcotic. My search for used books to fuel <a href="http://stungeye.com/archives/2003/11/time.php">Glutton University</a> was successful this month:
<ul>
<li>Language Truth and Logic - Alfred Jules Ayer - Read the condensed edition <a href="http://www.btinternet.com/~glynhughes/squashed/ayer.htm" target="_blank">here</a>. (Related: <a href="http://www.btinternet.com/~glynhughes/squashed/index.htm" target="_blank">More Squashed Philosophers</a>)</li>
<li>Intro To Logic - I. Copi &amp; C. Cohen - A first year philosophy Logic textbook. (Related: <a href="http://www-2.cs.cmu.edu/~michaelm/nidocs/Schroeder.pdf" target="_blank">How to tell a logical story</a> [pdf])</li>
<li>The Basic Writing of C. G. Jung - It's about time I read some <a href="http://www.cgjungpage.org/content/category/4/36/29/" target="_blank">Jung</a>.</li>
<li>The Origin of Consciousness in the Breakdown of the Bicameral Mind - Julian Jaynes - The introduction of which can be read <a href="http://members.shaw.ca/competitivenessofnations/Anno%20Jaynes%20Bicameral%20Mind1.htm" target="_blank">here</a>.</li>
<li>An Anthropologist on Mars - Oliver Sacks - Seven paradoxical tales of neurological disease - Excerpt here: <a href="http://kbs.cs.tu-berlin.de/~jutta/me/notes/hug.html" target="_blank">The hug machine</a>.</li>
<li>On Writing Well - William Zinsser - Excerpt here: <a href="http://www.usao.edu/~facbassitycb/clutter.htm" target="_blank">Clutter</a>.</li>
<li>The Algorithmic Beauty of Plants - P. Prusinkiewicz &amp; A. Lindenmayer - Can mathematical L-Systems define the structure of plants? (Related: <a href="http://www.cs.bgu.ac.il/~sipper/lsys/" target="_blank">L-System java applet</a>)</li>
<li>Images of Medicine - Edited by Jim Harter - 4800 historical medical engravings. (Related: <a href="http://clendening.kumc.edu/dc/rti/index.html" target="_blank">The medical images of the Clendening library</a>)</li></ul>
No more than 4 dollars was spent on each book. Avoid Amazon, support your local (used) book stores and your public library.
