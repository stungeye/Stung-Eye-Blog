The first set of images were taken at the <a href="http://www.gov.mb.ca/legtour/" target="_blank">Manitoba legislative building</a>.  The final image from that set shows a statue of local historical figure <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louis_Riel" target="_blank">Louis Riel</a>.  In 1995 this statue replaced an older Riel monument which (much like the history of Riel himself) was shrouded in controversy. (Related: <a href="http://www.uwo.ca/english/canadianpoetry/architexts/mnemographia_canadensis_2/essay_16.htm" target="_blank">Controversy in the Commemoration of Louis Riel</a>.)

<a href="http://www.gov.mb.ca" target="_blank">Manitoba</a> has a proud history of radical politics, including Louis Riel and the <a href="http://www.thecanadianencyclopedia.com/index.cfm?PgNm=ArchivedFeatures&amp;Params=A250" target="_blank">Winnipeg General Strike of 1919</a>, (considered by many to be Canada's most influencial general strike.)  (Related: <a href="http://www.mhs.mb.ca/docs/transactions/3/radicalpolitics.shtml" target="_blank">Radical Politics in Winnipeg: 1899-1915</a>.)
