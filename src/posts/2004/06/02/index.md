Are bloggers becoming instant pundits? (Related: Glenn Reynolds' <a href="http://instapundit.com/" target="_blank">Instapundit</a>)


Today I received an email from an associate producer from the <a href="http://www.cbc.ca" target="_blank">CBC</a>. Someone must have googled their way to <a href="http://stungeye.com/archives/2003/10/natures_climbin.php">my post on Sleep Paralysis</a>, while researching an upcoming feature for <a href="http://www.cbc.ca/nerve/" target="_blank">Nerve</a>.

A snippet from the email:

<I>"Right now I'm in the process of trying to put together a piece on sleep paralysis and I'm trying to locate people--in Canada and between 16-23--who have experienced sleep paralysis.  I'm hoping they can talk about it on camera and shed some light on the experience for our viewers.  Our show doesn't have a host and there are no producer voice-overs, meaning stories are told entirely by the people in the stories themselves.

I'm getting in touch with you because I'm hoping you might know of someone who fits this description or that you can point me in the direction of someone who can."</I>

I replied to express my interest in participating, even though I exceed the age-range by 3 year. 

If you fit the description, email me () and I will forward your info to the CBC.

Bill Werde from the New York Times and Douglas Wolk from the Village Voice <a href="http://stungeye.com/archives/2004/02/behind_her_smil.php">contacted me during Grey Tuesday</a>. If you blog it, they will come?
