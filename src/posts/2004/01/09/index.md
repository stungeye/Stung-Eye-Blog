The demo scene stream lead me to <a href="http://www.pouet.net/" target="_blank">Pouet.net</a>, a <i>scene</i> related resource centre. From there I discovered a real treat! (More info on that, later.)

Back in the early 90s, I was into the local <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulletin_board_system" target="_blank">BBS scene</a>. I witnessed the peak and the near-death of this digital phenom. Before the internet gained wide spread popularity, millions around the world were using their computer modems to connect over their phone lines to Electronic Bulletin board systems. There were even globally connected BBS networks like <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fidonet" target="_blank">Fidonet</a> that rivaled the early 90s internet in terms of ease of use,  community strength and content. Worldwide communities were formed that promoted the distribution of knowledge, creativity and the fellowship of man. Of these communities and scenes, I was drawn to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phreaking" target="_blank">phreaking</a>, <a href="http://en2.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hacking" target="_blank">hacking</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demo_scene" target="_blank">the demo and ansi-art scene</a>. I remember when my cousin first taught me how to make free long-distance modem calls through the Univerisity of Manitoba loopback switch. The world's BBS systems, like giant digital community built encyclopedias, were at my disposal.

This is where I learnt to love learning. Using the word "where" in this context is such an abstraction. For "where", was I while I soaked in this info from this proto-noosphere? I was at home. Sitting in front of my computer. And yet my consciousness was connecting to people and ideas around the world. Bringing 'home' some inspiration from a beautiful ANSI image created in Germany. Debating the finer points of shareware video games on a BBS in Hawaii.

And then I discovered the Net. Sure, sometimes I miss the BBS world, (especially it's lack of corporate sponsorship), but there's no turning back once you witness the future.

The treat I mentioned earlier is the final Artpack from the legendary <a href="http://www.everything2.com/index.pl?lastnode_id=19525&amp;node_id=385316" target="_blank">ANSI-art</a> crew, <a href="http://thisipis.mine.nu/acid/" target="_blank">Acid Productions</a>. These digital artists weave complex images with a limited colour palette and a collection of squares.
