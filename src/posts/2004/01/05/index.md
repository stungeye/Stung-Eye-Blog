I've got some links to hand out. Let's do this paragraph style instead of a bulleted list, this time around. The people at <a href="http://creativecommons.org/" target="_blank">creative commons</a> have launch <a href="http://creativecommons.org/projects/sampling" target="_blank">two sampling copyright licenses</a> free for public use. Much like <a href="http://creativecommons.org/learn/licenses/" target="_blank">their other public licenses</a>, the sample licenses will allow content creators to fuel the global creative process.

<i>"The Sampling licenses will help authors foster a broad range of culture, from photo collage to musical "mash-ups," that the law currently deems illegitimate. [...] The Sampling license will let authors invite others to transform their work, even for commercial purposes, while prohibiting distribution of verbatim copies, or any use in advertising.

For example, an artist could take a photo licensed under Sampling, crop it, and use it in a commercial collage, but she could not distribute simple copies of the whole, original photo. A DJ could borrow elements of a licensed song, royalty-free, and use them in an original piece. He could not, however, put a copy of the tune on a file-sharing network."</i>
