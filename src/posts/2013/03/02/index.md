[Winnipeg’s most perpetuated myth: Downtown is dangerous](http://www.spectatortribune.com/article/winnipegs-most-perpetuated-myth-downtown-is-dangerous/)

<p><a href="http://www.spectatortribune.com/article/winnipegs-most-perpetuated-myth-downtown-is-dangerous/">Brett Geisel argues</a> that &#8220;Winnipeg’s downtown is not only safe, and the suburban idea that it is dangerous is driven not by facts, but by racism.&#8221;</p>

<p>Plus a <a href="http://www.spectatortribune.com/article/winnipegs-most-perpetuated-myth-a-response/">thoughtful response by Kate Sjoberg</a>:</p>

<blockquote>
  <p>&#8220;Brett’s assertion that Winnipeg’s downtown is safe is only partly true, and raises issues that need more attention. Winnipeg’s downtown is safe depending on a number of social markers. It is most safe for white able bodied, heterosexual men between the ages of 20 and 60 who have a job and a place to live, whether they live there, commute in to work, or attend a hockey game.&#8221;</p>
</blockquote>
