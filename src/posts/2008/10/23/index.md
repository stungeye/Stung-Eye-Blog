&#8220;When they gulp air from the surface it gets forced into the labyrinth organ. In the labyrinth there are lots of small maze-like compartments of thin boney plates called lamellae.

<p>Our <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Betta">Betta</a> fish has a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Labyrinth_organ#Labyrinth_organ">Labyrinth organ</a>, meaning it can breath air from the surface of the water.</p>

<p>&#8220;<em>When they gulp air from the surface it gets forced into the labyrinth organ. In the labyrinth there are lots of small maze-like compartments of thin boney plates called lamellae.</em></p>

<p><em>The lamellae are covered with membranes. Blood passes through the membranes and the oxygen is absorbed into the bloodstream.</em></p>

<p><em>If a labyrinth fish ends up out of the water it can stay alive for a long time if it stays moist. In fact they can even crawl across land to a different body of water if they have to.</em>&#8221; -<a href="http://freshaquarium.about.com/cs/qa/l/blwill0003.htm">source</a></p>
