[the ad hominem fallacy fallacy](http://plover.net/~bonds/adhominem.html)

<p>Debates on the internet tend towards <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flame_war">flame wars</a>; however, <em>&#8220;if you can&#8217;t demonstrate that your opponent is trying to counter your argument by attacking you, you can&#8217;t demonstrate that he is resorting to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ad_hominem">ad hominem</a>.&#8221;</em></p>

<p><em>&#8220;Those who are quick to squeal &#8216;ad hominem&#8217; are often guilty of several other logical fallacies, including one of the worst of all: the fallacious belief that introducing an impressive-sounding Latin term somehow gives one the decisive edge in an argument.&#8221;</em></p>

<p>A might be wrong, but B&#8217;s reply is not Ad Hominem:</p>

<ul>
<li>
<strong>A:</strong> &#8220;All rodents are mammals, but a weasel isn&#8217;t a rodent, so it can&#8217;t be a mammal.&#8221;</li>
<li>
<strong>B:</strong> &#8220;You&#8217;re an asshole.&#8221;</li>
</ul>
