## Exit the Loop, but When?

<p>There is a problem-solving strategy in computer science called <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Recursion">recursion</a>. It amounts to programming sets of instructions that are defined in terms of themselves (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Self-referential">self-referential</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meta">meta</a>).</p>

<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Douglas_Hofstadter">Douglas Hofstadter</a>&#8217;s academic pursuits centre on the connection between recursion and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Consciousness">consciousness</a>. Hofstadter maintains that our brains are recursive in nature, our consciousness born amongst the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strange_loops">strange loops</a> of our thoughts about thinking.</p>

<p>A <a href="http://mail.pm.org/pipermail/sanfrancisco-pm/2006-March/000438.html">recursive definition of recursion</a>: <em>&#8220;If you already know what recursion is, just remember the answer. Otherwise, find someone who is standing closer to Douglas Hofstadter than you are; then ask him or her what recursion is.&#8221;</em></p>

<p>Linguist <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Noam_Chomsky">Noam Chomsky</a> has theorized that there is no limit to the expressive nature of English (and other similarly structured languages) due to the recursive device of embedding sentences in sentences.</p>

<p><em>This is the man all tattered and torn,<br/>
That kissed the maiden all forlorn,<br/>
That milked the cow with the crumpled horn,<br/>
That tossed the dog,<br/>
That worried the cat,<br/>
That killed the rat,<br/>
That ate the malt<br/>
That lay in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.or/wiki/The_House_That_Jack_Built">the house that Jack built</a>.</em></p>

<p>This post was inspired by <a href="http://www.stevelosh.com/blog/2008/8/29/beauty-in-computer-science-recursion.html">a blog on beauty in computer science</a>. My favourite recursively defined <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fractal">fractal</a> is the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierpinski_triangle">Sierpiński triangle</a>. I enjoy writing <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Recursion_%28computer_science%29">recursive algorithms</a>.</p>
