<p>On October 4th of this year the people of Manitoba go to the polls to elect their next Legislative Assembly and Premier.</p>

<p>Making an informed choice for your vote requires that you know the candidates, their platforms and the issues they discuss.</p>

<p><strong>This is why we built <a href="http://manitobaelection.ca">ManitobaElection.ca</a></strong>, a citizen created website for the upcoming Manitoba provincial election.</p>

<p>Currently you can view all the <a href="http://manitobaelection.ca/constituencies">constituencies</a>, <a href="http://manitobaelection.ca/candidates">candidate nominees</a> and the <a href="http://manitobaelection.ca/candidates/incumbents">incumbents</a>. We have plenty more in the works including interactive maps, historical election results, candidate news feeds, an events calendar, candidate questionnaires, and educational resources.</p>

<p>Your feedback is much appreciated. Send your comments and suggestions to <a href="mailto:manitoba.election@gmail.com">manitoba.election@gmail.com</a> or <a href="http://twitter.com/#!/mbelection">@MbElection on Twitter</a>.</p>

<p>* * *</p>

<p><a href="http://manitobaelection.ca">ManitobaElection.ca</a> was created in the spirit of openness, community, accountability and communication. In 2010 we created a similar website for the <a href="http://winnipegelection.ca">Winnipeg election</a> which helped over 40,000 Winnipeggers research their mayoral, city council and school trustee candidates. After the election <a href="http://winnipegelection.ca/open_data_2010">we released an open data collection</a> including the website source, ward map polygons, and historical election results.</p>
