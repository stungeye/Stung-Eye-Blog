[Do numbers exist?](http://blog.hiremebecauseimsmart.com/post/2747507384/quine)

<blockquote>
  <p>You explain to me what 4 is by pointing to four rocks on the ground, or dropping them in succession &#8212; Peano map, Peano map, Peano map, Peano map. Sure. But that’s an <strong>example</strong> of the number 4, not the number 4 itself.</p>
  
  <p>So is it even possible to say what a [whole] number is?</p>
  
  <p>Willard van Orman <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/W._V._Quine">Quine</a> had an interesting answer. He said that the number seventeen &#8220;is&#8221; the equivalence class of all sets of with 17 elements.</p>
  
  <p>Accept that or not, it’s at least a good try. Whether or not numbers actually exist, we can use math to figure things out. <a href="http://blog.hiremebecauseimsmart.com/post/2747507384/quine">&#8230;</a></p>
</blockquote>
