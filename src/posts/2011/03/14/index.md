<blockquote>
  <p>The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kirkbride_Plan">Kirkbride plan</a> for mental hospitals consists of sets of enormous, symmetrical, staggered wings, like a bat made out of lego. Men are on the left and women on the right in sections that radiate from the main entrance for increasingly violent or incurable patients.</p>
  
  <p>Early mental institutions where patients had to pay for their own incarceration would also vary in class (rich to poor) on the y axis. The staggering of the wings ensured the flow of air through each, purging them of diseased vapors perhaps, such was the Victorian obsession with fresh air.</p>
</blockquote>
