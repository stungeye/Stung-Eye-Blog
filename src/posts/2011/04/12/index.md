<p>This Saturday (April 16th) I’ll be giving a talk on <strong>chemical etching</strong> at the <a href="http://www.skullspace.ca/wiki/index.php/Hack-a-thon_5">5th Skullspace Hack-a-thon</a>.</p>

<p><em>Etching is the process of using corrosive chemicals to cut into the unprotected parts of an object’s surface.</em></p>

<p>Etching has both <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Etching">artistic</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Industrial_etching">industrial</a> uses. I have experience with both types of etching and I’ll be sharing my knowledge of:</p>

<ul><li>The History of Etching.</li>
<li>Etched Copper and the <a href="http://allangeske.ca/node/12">Printing Press</a>. (Printmaking)</li>
<li>Etching Circuit Boards for Electronic Prototyping.</li>
</ul><p>I’ll see you there.</p>

<p>In the meanwhile, check out <a href="http://allangeske.ca/image/tid/3">the etchings of my father, Allan Geske</a>.</p>
