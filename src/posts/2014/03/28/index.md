## SHE, HE, THEY

<p>THEY had just met.</p>

<p>HE, wearing suit, tie; briefcase in hand.<br/>
SHE, wearing flower-print dress, necklace; purse in hand.</p>

<p>"You remind me", says HE, "of you".<br/>
&#8220;So I am told&#8221;, says SHE, &#8220;by you&#8221;.</p>

<p>THEY begin to walk.<br/>
HE, holding HIS briefcase like it was HER hand.<br/>
SHE, holding HER purse like it was HIS hand.<br/>
THEY walk without speaking for some time. Hand in hand in mind.</p>

<p>HE opens HIS mouth to say something. Nothing comes out.<br/>
SHE sees HIS open mouth and it makes HER yawn.</p>

<p>"Look!", says SHE as SHE points.<br/>
THEY watch as a crane lowers a steeple onto a now finished church.<br/>
&#8220;Complete&#8221;, says HE.</p>

<p>THEY play at being cranes. What fun it is to dream of strength and amazement.</p>

<p>"Do you think that you might love me?", says SHE.<br/>
&#8220;How can that be?&#8221;, says HE.<br/>
&#8220;Love at first sight&#8221;, says SHE.</p>

<p>Silence. Deep breathes. Pupils widen. Corners of lips curl.</p>

<p>"What does love feel like?", says HE.<br/>
&#8220;Like the opposite of a stomach ache&#8221;, says SHE, &#8220;only more pleasant.&#8221;</p>

<p>"I feel full", says HE, "but I think that is lunch."</p>

<p>THEY play at being lovers.</p>

<p>What fun it is to dream.</p>
