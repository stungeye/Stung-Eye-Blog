<p>I read nineteen books this past year, one more than in <a href="http://stungeye.com/archive/by_date/2012/12/31">2012</a>, and three more than <a href="http://stungeye.com/archive/by_date/2011/12/18">2011</a>.  Only one of the books was read on my Kobo, the rest were deadtree.</p>

<ul><li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Confusion">The Confusion</a> - Neal Stephenson</li>
<li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Travels_in_the_Scriptorium">Travels in the Scriptorium</a> - Paul Auster</li>
<li><a href="http://www.goodreads.com/book/show/3833.The_Black_Tower">The Black Tower</a> - P.D. James</li>

<li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lila:_An_Inquiry_into_Morals">Lila</a> - Robert M. Pirsig  </li>
<li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_Feast_for_Crows">A Feast of Crows</a> - George R.R. Martin</li>
<li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Outliers_%28book%29">Outliers</a> -  Malcolm Gladwell</li>
<li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apprentice_Adept">Split Infinity</a> - Piers Anthony</li>
<li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inferno_%28Dan_Brown_novel%29">Inferno</a> - Dan Brown</li>
<li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Snow_Crash">Snow Crash</a> - Neal Stephenson</li>
<li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virtual_Light">Virtual Light</a> - William Gibson</li>
<li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Yiddish_Policemen's_Union">The Yiddish Policemen’s Union</a> - Michael Chabon</li>
<li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Man's_Search_for_Meaning">Man’s Search for Meaning</a> - Viktor Frankl</li>
<li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Alchemist_%28novel%29">The Alchemist</a> - Paulo Coelho (re-read)</li>
<li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dirk_Gently's_Holistic_Detective_Agency">Dirk Gently’s Holistic Detective Agency</a> - Douglas Adams</li>
<li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Diamond_Age">The Diamond Age</a> - Neal Stephenson</li>
<li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inheritance_%28novel%29">Inheritance</a> - Christopher Paolini</li>
<li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bluebeard_%28Vonnegut_novel%29">Bluebeard</a> - Kurt Vonnegut</li>
<li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Westing_Game">The Westing Game</a> - Ellen Raskin</li>
</ul><p>Read in this order. The only dud was Piers Anthony’s Split Infinity. The best of the lot were the three Stephenson novels (fan boy alert!) and Viktor Frankl’s search for meaning. Adams and Vonnegut provided the laughs. Following Snow Crash with Virtual Light highlighted the shared themes of corporate/religious nation-states, virtual worlds, and oddly, messenger culture. Zen and Lila remain fascinating fourteen years later, although I’ve switched which of the two I prefer.</p>
