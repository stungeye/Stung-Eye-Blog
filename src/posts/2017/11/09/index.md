<p><a href="https://twitter.com/chefquix">Andrew Burton</a> and I spent the morning talking Open Data and Open Government with these passionate public servants at <a href="http://canadabeyond150.ca/themes-methods-en.html">Canada Beyond 150</a>&rsquo;s three day conference in Winnipeg.</p>

<blockquote>
  <p>&ldquo;Canada Beyond 150 is a ten-month professional development program involving a Canada-wide group of early-career public servants. The project is designed to support leadership and skills development, and to drive a culture shift across the federal public service.&rdquo;</p>
</blockquote>

<p>As part of this program, teams of public servants are working together to explore policy challenges including: <a href="http://canadabeyond150.ca/themes-methods-en.html#theme5">reconciliation</a>, <a href="http://canadabeyond150.ca/themes-methods-en.html#theme1">open and transparent government</a>, <a href="http://canadabeyond150.ca/themes-methods-en.html#theme3">sustainable development</a>, <a href="http://canadabeyond150.ca/themes-methods-en.html#theme2">feminist government</a>, and <a href="http://canadabeyond150.ca/themes-methods-en.html#theme4">socio-economic inclusion</a>.</p>

<p>Andrew and I meet with the open and transparent government team. Andrew was there in his capacity as the <a href="https://data.winnipeg.ca/">City of Winnipeg Open Data</a> manager. I was there as executive directory of <a href="http://opendemocracymanitoba.ca/">Open Democracy Manitoba</a>.</p>

<p>We discussed the evolution (and/or disappearance) of privacy, the implications of a shift to digital government services, proactive vs reactive public disclosure, IT procurement, open source goverance, fake new, policy making as a participatory act, algorithmic biases, the logistics of open data, citizen engagement, artificial intelligence, techno-privilege, trust and reputation online, and so much more.</p>

<p>I was honoured to be invited to share my experiences and perspectives on these topics. It was inspiring morning. I look forward to seeing what kinds of improvements and innovations these folks will help bring to our federal public service.</p>

<p>Oh, and in case you&rsquo;re curious, here&rsquo;s <a href="http://canadabeyond150.ca/blog/?p=103">why they chose Winnipeg as the location for their mid-project conference</a>.</p>
