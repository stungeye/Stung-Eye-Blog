Today's flash experiment deals with streaming video. I've decided to showcase a short clip from Richard Linklater's <a href="http://us.imdb.com/title/tt0243017/" target="_blank">Waking Life</a>.  In this clip, Julie Delpy and Ethan Hawke reprise their roles as Celine and Jesse from another Linklater film, <a href="http://us.imdb.com/title/tt0112471/" target="_blank">Before Sunrise</a>.

This movie clip deals with some interesting ideas: Past lives, collective memory, dream consciousness and more.

On the technical side of things, the clip was taken from a DIVX rip of the Waking life DVD. Since flash doesn't support the DIVX codec I had to use <a href="http://fullart.topcities.com/aviutl.htm" target="_blank">AVIUTL</a> to capture the clip and convert it to a supported AVI codec.  The conversion process left the audio and video out of sync. To fix this, I imported both the audio and video separately into Cubase SX where I re-sync them. I then noticed that although you can import video into Cubase SX, you can't export it! I had to export the re-sync'ed audio and use <a href="http://www.virtualdub.org/" target="_blank">Virtual Dub</a> to add it to the video clip. I then imported the resultant 118MB audio/video clip into Flash MX where it was compress down to a 14MB flash stream. What a maze!
