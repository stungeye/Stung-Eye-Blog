<a href="http://theeducatedmachine.com/sublimate/" target="_blank">Mike</a> and I just wrapped up a sonic experiment. Pong, the electronic music equivalent of <a href="http://www.coudal.com/tennis.php" target="_blank">Photoshop tennis</a>.

Starting with <a href="http://www.stungeye.com/sounds/pong/bassy.mp3">this bass loop</a>, "played, recorded, and 'verbed out by" Mike, we volleyed back and forth. Adding here. Tweaking there.

After six iterations we were left with this: <a href="http://www.stungeye.com/sounds/pong/blip_blop.mp3">Blip Blop - A Collaborative Audio Collage</a>.

<a href="http://www.ncf.ca/~ek867/wood_s_lot.html" target="_blank">Wood S Lot</a> introduced me to the wonderful <a href="http://artchive.com/ftp_site.htm" target="_blank">Artchive</a> with a link to <a href="http://www.artchive.com/artchive/K/kandinsky.html" target="_blank">this write-up on Wassily Kandinsky</a>.

<i>"[Wassily Kandinsky's] 'inner necessity' to express his emotional perceptions led to the development of an abstract style of painting that was based on the non-representational properties of color and form. Kandinsky's compositions were the culmination of his efforts to create a "pure painting" that would provide the same emotional power as a musical composition."</i>

Kandinsky's artistic exploration of musical tools, such as melody and rhythm, to create abstract visual "compositions" can be seen in the following thumbnail of his <i>Composition VII</i>. Study the thumbnail before you click to reveal a larger image. Notice the melodic visual structures coupled with the unstructured narrative and the rhythmic nature of the entire piece.

<a href="#" border="0" onmouseover="window.status='Click to view Kandinsky - Composition VII in another window';return true" onmouseout="window.status='';return true" onclick="display_image('http://www.stungeye.com/images/others/1203/kandinsky_L.jpg','Kandinsky - Composition VII',640,426);return false;"><img hspace="0" vspace="0" border="0" src="http://www.stungeye.com/images/others/1203/kandinsky_T.jpg"></a>

<i>"The creation of [Composition VII] involved over thirty preparatory drawings, watercolors and oil studies. Amazingly, once he had completed the preparatory work, Kandinsky executed the actual painting of Composition VII in less than four days."</i>

The rest of the Composition series can be seen <a href="http://www.glyphs.com/art/kandinsky/" target="_blank">here</a>. Interestingly, the Artchive write-up states that Kandinsky saw <a href="http://www.artchive.com/artchive/C/cezanne/bathers.jpg.html" target="_blank">Cezanne's Large Bathers</a> as <i>"an example of this clearly laid out, melodic composition with open rhythms."</i>
