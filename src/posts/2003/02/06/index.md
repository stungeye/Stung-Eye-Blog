One of my favourite hip-hop producers, <a href="http://www.13stars.org/" target="_blank">Sixtoo</a>, has signed a <a href="http://www.chartattack.com/DAMN/2003/01/2824.cfm" target="_blank">three record deal</a> with <a href="http://www.allmusic.com/cg/amg.dll?p=amg&amp;opt1=1&amp;sql=coldcut" target="_blank">Coldcut</a>’s record label <a href="http://www.ninjatune.net/home/" target="_blank">Ninja Tune</a>. This news comes only months after his Sebutones partner in crime, <a href="http://www.buck65.com" target="_blank">Buck 65</a>,  released his entire <a href="http://www.buck65square.com" target="_blank">Language Arts series</a> through Warner Canada.

<a href="http://www.ugsmag.com/" target="_blank">Underground sound magazine</a> is runnning <a href="http://www.ugsmag.com/004/0405.html" target="_blank">an interview</a> with my current fav. MC/Producer, <a href="http://www.allmusic.com/cg/amg.dll?p=amg&amp;opt1=1&amp;sql=j-live" target="_blank">J-Live</a>. Their online magazine looks great design-wise and contains some very dope interviews and music reviews. (Check the archives)

The hip-hop edition of the <a href="http://www.digyourroots.ca/" target="_blank">Dig Your Roots</a> compilation will hit the streets on March 3rd, featuring some amazing Canadian underground talent, (including Winnipeg’s own <a href="http://www.freksho.com/" target="_blank">Frek Sho</a>). The DYR site also features <a href="http://www.digyourroots.ca/dyr.php" target="_blank">many fine tracks</a> that didn’t make the cut for the CD.

Still on the hip-hop tip, the <a href="http://www.peanutsandcorn.com" target="_blank">Break Bread crew</a> will be performing at the <a href="http://www.wecc.ca" target="_blank">West End</a> on February 14th as part of the <a href="http://www.ckuw.org" target="_blank">CKUW</a> Fundrive, along with The Panty Apples, RWPO, and the Giant Sons.

Keeping it close to home, check out the <a href="http://www.kickittilitbreaks.com/" target="_blank">Kick It Till It Breaks</a> site for some really nice political, music oriented, and skateboarding themed photographs. Or surf over to the newly designed <a href="http://www.videopool.org" target="_blank">Video Pool webpage</a>. Video Pool is a nonprofit Artist-Run Centre dedicated to independent video, audio and computer integrated multimedia production, located here in Winnipeg.
