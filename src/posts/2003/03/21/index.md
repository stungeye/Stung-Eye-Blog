When the cable company turned off my cable TV service they left me with <a href="http://www.cnn.com" target="_blank">CNN</a>. Portions of last night’s war coverage felt eerily similar to a reality TV show or to the manic coverage of an exciting hockey game. While showing off their latest live coverage gadgets some the reporters could not contain their excitement. I’m not sure if CNN actually has a reporter on board a US tank in the front lines or just a video camera, but either way, the reporters have taken to using ‘we’ to describe the army/media hybrid they have create. ‘As you can see, <i>we</i> are increasing our speeds now and <i>we</i> should arrive at the outskirts of Baghdad sometime tomorrow.’
 
On Wednesday, The New York Post encouraged readers to boycott movies starring "appeasement-loving celebs." A Fox News anchor, recently said people who continued to protest against the war once it begins would be "un-American." (More info <a href="http://www.globeandmail.com/servlet/ArticleNews/TPStory/LAC/20030320/UAMERN/TPInternational/TopStories" target="_blank">here</a>.)

And to top it all off, America's largest radio conglomerate Clear Channel, <a href="http://www.commondreams.org/headlines03/0319-01.htm" target="_blank">has been holding pro-war rallies</a>.

How’s that for unbiased news coverage.
