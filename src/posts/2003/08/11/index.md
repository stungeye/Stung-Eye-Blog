Backwards in time across the zones we use to section up our world.
So is time really distance through space? Yes and no.<BR>
It’s more like the fusion of distance and memory.
Saw some old friends, they asked: "Hey do you remember me?"<BR>
Played amongst the wise old cedars.
Fed my mind from the top like old fashion bird feeders.<BR>
I’m back and I’m almost at a loss for words.
Almost.<BR>
Either this city has changed or else I have: Most likely both. 
The only thing permanent is impermanence.<BR>
If you suffer, it is not because things are impermanent.
It is because you believe things are not.<BR>
We like to make believe that we are individuals.
How dare you choose to blind your third eye when we thrive on the inner visuals?<BR><BR>
Cupped hands form an hourglass when filled with sand<BR><BR>
More sand falls than can be caught
It passes through fingers, leaving my stomach in knots<BR> 
A friend comments: <BR> 
"Those sand grains were once stones, see?
 They are now finer for having known thee."<BR> 
Isn't it amazing how positive spins bring grins?
It isn't all that phasing; now causative sins sting skin<BR> 
It's the sun's sin that now stings this idle skin<BR> 
Sitting on a beach made from all these used up memories
This grain's ancestor was granite - That one came from emery<BR> 
How many years will pass until no one remembers me?
I'll be great-great-grandpa 'what's-his-name' hung from the family tree.<BR> 
Like a stone pulled down by gravity through the water of a stream
I have certain goals that call me so loudly that I can't see the forest for the screams<BR> 
You could say that I'm a seeker. You could say I'm building steam.
I see time as man's invention. (Is this really all a dream?)
