Who remembers <a href="http://wwwusers.imaginet.fr/~mhp/" target="_blank">Barbapapa</a>? An amorphous pink creature who spawned a family of colourful shape-shifting blobs with the help of his partner Barbamama. The brainchild of Annette Tison, a french architect, and Talus Taylor, an American biology/mathematics teacher, the <a href="http://www.naughtykitty.org/barbapapa/barbabooks.html" target="_blank">Barba-family</a> was something of a 70s phenomenon. I can distinctly remember wishing I could become a shape-shifting blob myself or at least find one <a href="http://www.naughtykitty.org/barbapapa/barbapapabk.html" target="_blank">growing my garden</a>.<BR>

<a href="http://www.authorama.com/book.php?title=the-sorrows-of-young-werther&part=1" target="_blank">The Sorrows of Young Werther</a> was published anonymously in 1774 by 24 year old Johann Wolfgang von Goethe; it caused a lot of people in Germany and elsewhere to commit suicide.
