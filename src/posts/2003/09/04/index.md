<a href="http://www.xml.com/pub/a/2002/12/18/dive-into-xml.html" target="_blank">RSS</a>. <B>R</B>eally <B>S</B>imple <B>S</B>yndication is an <a href="http://www.w3.org/XML/" target="_blank">XML</a> template used, amongst other things, to syndicate the content of news or news-like web-sites. Blogs fall under the category of news-like sites and some of your favourite blogs most likely already support RSS feeds. Have you ever seen an orange XML icon displayed on a blog or news site? That is a link to their RSS feed.<BR>
A RSS feed allows the content of a webpage to be easily <a href="http://www.newsisfree.com/?page=1" target="_blank">syndicated to another page</a> or to be read via a <a href="http://blogs.salon.com/0001429/stories/2002/12/10/blogsAndNewsAggregatorsWit.html" target="_blank">RSS news aggregator</a>. More and more information is being added daily to the net and we need a better way to sort through it all. News aggregation lets you monitor all your favorite RSS enabled web-pages for updates. Not really a big deal if you skim through 2 or 3 pages daily, but what if one day you need to skim <a href="http://www.dashes.com/anil/index.php?archives/005631.php" target="_blank">10,000 blogs on a daily basis</a>?<BR>
There are lots of free RSS news aggregators out there. I'm currently testing out a web-based tool called <a href="http://www.disobey.com/amphetadesk/" target="_blank">Amphetadesk</a> and a windows based tool called <a href="http://www.awasu.com/index.php" target="_blank">Awasu</a>. (If you're a Mac user check out <a href="http://ranchero.com/netnewswire/" target="_blank">NetNewsWire Lite</a>.) I'll soon be adding a RSS feed to this site using <a href="http://www.blogger.com/rss_with_blogger.pyra" target="_blank">this method via blogger</a>. Much thanks to Chris over at Anti-Mega for explaining <a href="http://undergroundlondon.com/antimega/archives/000357.html" target="_blank">the importance of RSS for blogs</a>.<BR>
