<p>Here’s a snapshot of my exercise achievements in 2024:</p><p>I covered 2,100km last year, splitting my cardio between running and cycling. Running accounted for 900 km, mostly along the Harte Trail and throughout Assiniboine Forest. I run outdoors throughout the summer and the winter. Most of my runs ranged from 5 to 10km, with two standout runs hitting half-marathon distance (21 km). Cycling made up the remaining 1,200km, largely from my fair-weather bike commutes to work. It&rsquo;s a 14km journey each way to the Red River College downtown campus.</p><p>Winter running in Winnipeg requires some special care. After fracturing my wrist in 2017 from slipping on ice in Assiniboine Park, I started adding <a href="https://www.koldkutter.com/">Kold Kutter motorsport ice-racing screws</a> to my running shoes for better winter traction. They are incredibly effective — I&rsquo;ve had zero slips since then.</p><p>I also kept up a strength training routine in 2024, working out 2 to 3 times per week throughout the year, even while managing a minor injury (more on that below). These home workout sessions include a mix of dumbbells, a Total Gym 1000, and body-weight exercises.</p><p>My strength training routines were developed with ChatGPT back in 2023 — yes, I&rsquo;m even a nerd when it comes to exercise. I fed in all my available equipment and asked for &ldquo;a full-body, time-efficient and highly effective strength training plan.&rdquo; Since starting this program in July 2023, I’ve completed over 180 sessions. They consist of three focused workouts: chest and shoulders, back and biceps, and legs and shoulders.</p><p>In an effort to preserve the strength and mobility of my knees, I&rsquo;ve also incorporated the <a href="https://www.atgonlinecoaching.com/articles/article-knee-ability-zero-basics-and-pro">Knees Over Toes Zero protocol</a> into my weekly routine. So far so good! It&rsquo;s especially fun to see the surprise on people&rsquo;s faces when I&rsquo;m running backwards on the Harte Trail.</p><p>In early 2024, I noticed discomfort in my lower left bicep during workouts. Over time, the discomfort progressed to pain, which I later identified as distal bicep tendonitis. My initial approach — stopping bicep exercises and waiting for it to heal — proved ineffective even after three months of rest.</p><p>To address this, I began a re-strengthening protocol using light weights, beginning with just 3-pound dumbbells and gradually working up to 20 pounds over four months. The recovery program included short bicep curl sessions twice daily, exercises with a <a href="https://www.theraband.com/products/flexbar">Theraband Flexbar</a> (a specialized tool for tendon rehabilitation), and <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RVWjVSQXsl4">voodoo flossing</a>, which helps improve blood/nutrient flow and mobility.</p><p>Most exercises are now pain-free, but there&rsquo;s still some minor discomfort on bicep days. I still haven&rsquo;t returned to pull-ups or chin-ups, preferring to stick with assisted versions of these exercises on the Total Gym while continuing my recovery.</p><p>My goal for 2025 is to hit 2,000km again for running and cycling, and to continue the full-body workouts and knee protocol. Shout out to Shan, my exercise inspiration. She doesn&rsquo;t track all her exercise like I do, but probably tripled or quadrupled my stats or more! :) </p>
