I think, therefore I am really not there.”</i></li></ul><h2>Top Three Books of 2024</h2><p><b>This is How You Lose The Time War - Amal El-Mohtar and Max Gladstone</b></p><blockquote class="npf_indented"><p>You ask if I’ve been lonely. I hardly know how to answer. I have observed friendship as one observes high holy days: breathtakingly short, whirlwinds of intimate endeavour, frenzied carousing, the sharing of food, of wine, of honey. Compressed, always, and gone as soon as they come. It is often my duty to fall in love convincingly, and certainly I’ve received no complaints. But that is work, and there are better things of which to write.</p></blockquote><p><i>Burn before reading.</i> A co-written epistolary novella that blends espionage, time travel, poetry, and romance. Lent to me by a student. It left me wanting more, but any longer and the letter writing conceit may have worn thin.</p><p><b>The Playful Game Design Process - Richard Lemarchand</b></p><blockquote class="npf_indented"><p>Imagination and design are closely connected. The dreams we dream at night and by day can lead to the greatest accomplishments in art and literature, science and technology, industry and entertainment. But until we make decisions and act upon them, we   are not designing, only speculating.</p></blockquote><p>A non-technical handbook on producing video games with a focus on curiosity, flexibility, generosity, humility, and respect. It has already had a profound impact on how I run the Game Studio courses in the <a href="https://www.rrc.ca/explore/program/game-development-programming/">Red River College Polytech Game Development program</a>.</p><p><b>Co-Intelligence: Living and Working with AI - Ethan Mollick</b></p><blockquote class="npf_indented"><p>You should try inviting AI to help you in everything you do, barring legal or ethical barriers. As you experiment, you may find that AI help can be satisfying, or frustrating, or useless, or unnerving. But you aren’t just doing this for help alone; familiarizing yourself with AI’s capabilities allows you to better understand how it can assist you — or threaten you and your job.</p></blockquote><p>Ethan Mollick, Professor of Management at U-Penn’s Wharton School, encourages us to experiment widely with Ai to explore the limitations and possibilities of this evolving technology.</p><p>Ai can mean many things. This book focuses on Ai chat systems built on <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lnA9DMvHtfI">Large Language Machine-Learning Models</a>, which generate text by re-purposing patterns found in their vast training data. These models have been trained on an enormous volume of text — drawn from books, articles, and the public internet — shaping the model’s ability to mimic human responses.</p><p>If you haven’t spent time playing around with generative Ai, I’d recommend <a href="https://claude.ai/">Claude for text-based chats</a> and <a href="https://pi.ai/">Pi for voice conversations</a>.</p><h2>Runners-Up</h2><ul><li>Birdsall’s Penderwicks series continues to captivate the girls. Read these to your kids!</li><li>Tchaikovsky entire Children of Time series is a must-read for sci-fi lovers.</li><li>Le Guin’s masterpiece A Wizard of Earthsea might seem full of fantasy tropes, until you realize she create those tropes with this book.</li><li>All teacher’s (regardless of grade level) should read Waitzkin’s The Art of Learning and Burgess’ Teach Like a Pirate.</li><li>Camfield’s We Can Do Better is an accessible introduction to Marxist reconstructed historical materialism. From the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sara_R._Farris">Sara Farris</a> quote on the cover: “For anyone who wants to understand the complex forces that shape our society and change them.”</li></ul>
