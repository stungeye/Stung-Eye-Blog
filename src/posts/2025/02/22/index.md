<p><b>Today’s drop is a hip hop mixtape of my fav 15 releases from Manitoban rappers and beat makers last year.</b></p><p>I’ve been readying <a href="https://www.grittycitywpg.com/">Gritty City: An Oral History of Winnipeg Hip-Hop Music: 1980-2005</a> by Nigel Webber, a deep-dive into local hip-hop history. That’s right, Manitoba has been head-bobbing to beats and rhymes for over 40 years. I found the scene 25 years ago and have been a fan ever since. The talent here always blows my mind.</p><p>Back in December I listened to 300 tracks released in 2024 by Manitoba artists and curated three playlists. The first playlist was <a href="https://www.stungeye.com/archive/by_date/2025/02/15">17 slices of Manitoba Party Music</a>. The second is this mix of 15 MB hip hop tracks from across the province.</p><p> &#128680; Explicit lyrics. Listen here or in the embedded players below:</p><ul><li><a href="https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLHwGpEAs-3LPMOnwVKeDxaAl1RM291TTv">Toba Hop 2024 Mixtape on YouTube</a></li><li><a href="https://open.spotify.com/playlist/7ioVSRLuftquwkDqcR8gaj?si=43cb2e8d22244c7a">Toba Hop 2024 Mixtape on Spotify</a></li></ul><p>The playlist features Manitoba hip hop OGs, The Gumshoe Strut, Charlie Fettah, and The Lytics. I first heard Strut on the Milch & Allegra tape in 2000, and both The Lytics (Big City Soundgirl!) and Fettah (as part of Winnipeg’s Most) in 2010.</p><p>You’ll find familiar names like Anthony OKS and Super Duty Tough Work (with Cadence Weapon!), a wave of up-and-comers, fire tracks from Northern Manitoba, and Indigenous talent making moves.</p><p>One more MB 2024 playlist to come. It's eclectic!</p><p>Catch you on the flip side, grinners.</p>
