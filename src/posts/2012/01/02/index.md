[Flaws in the 2010 Winnipeg Police Annual Report](http://mennozacharias.com/2011/12/29/flaws-in-the-2010-winnipeg-police-annual-report/)

<p><em>&#8220;There are three kinds of lies: lies, damned lies, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lies,_damned_lies,_and_statistics">statistics</a>.&#8221;</em></p>

<p>Looks like the Winnipeg Police Service needs bone up on their math skills. <a href="http://www.winnipeg.ca/police/annualreports/2010/2010_wps_annual_report_english.pdf">Their 2010 annual report</a> includes some strange stats. When comparing 2010 with 2009:</p>

<ul><li>They report a 20-30% decrease in crime per district, even though <a href="http://mennozacharias.com/2011/12/31/flaws-in-the-2010-winnipeg-police-annual-report-part-2/">their reported city-wide decrease is only 7%</a>.</li>
<li>This 7% decrease, when compared with their actual reported offence numbers, <a href="http://mennozacharias.com/2011/12/29/flaws-in-the-2010-winnipeg-police-annual-report/">turns out to be a 9% increase</a>.</li>
<li>They report a decreases in &#8220;Events for Service&#8221; for all districts, even though <a href="http://mennozacharias.com/2012/01/01/flaws-in-the-2010-winnipeg-police-annual-report-part-iii/">there was no significant city-wide change in &#8220;Events for Service&#8221; between the two years</a>.</li>
</ul><p>Some of these discrepancies appear to be tied to the new WPS policy of listing certain incidents as &#8220;non-district specific.&#8221;</p>
