[I’m Not a “Curator”](http://www.marco.org/2012/03/12/not-a-curator)

<p>A response to <a href="http://www.curatorscode.org/">the curator&#8217;s code</a>, an attempt to codify and standardize “via” links and attribution from link blogs and aggregators.</p>

<p>I usually try to provide &#8220;via&#8221; links here on stungeye, but I don&#8217;t believe online discovery should <em>require</em> attribution.</p>

<blockquote>
  <p>&#8220;The value of authorship is much more clear. But regardless of how much time it takes to find interesting links every day, I don’t think most intermediaries deserve credit for simply sharing a link to someone else’s work.&#8221;</p>
</blockquote>

<p>Also, from <a href="http://www.thingsmagazine.net/?p=6747">Infinite Monkeys and Infinite Links</a>:</p>

<blockquote>
  <p>&#8220;The idea of taking the relatively rigid and formal conventions of academic attribution and translating them to a website doesn’t sit very well with us. It’s not just that we’re lazy, but that the lack of proper attribution is not the same as plagiarism or copyright violation; no-one is losing out financially by not being credited with an online ‘discovery’.&#8221;</p>
</blockquote>
