## Learning in Public

<p>I teach coding at <a href="https://www.rrc.ca/informationsystems/">Red River College</a>. I&rsquo;ve been at the college for almost 12.5. Although I&rsquo;ve learned <strong>a lot</strong> over this time, this learning has been almost exclusivly related to teaching and web development (and outside of work, parenting and <a href="http://opendemocracymanitoba.ca">open government</a>).</p>

<p>It&rsquo;s time to step a bit of my comfort zone: <strong>I&rsquo;m going to learn modern game development.</strong></p>

<p>So, okay, it&rsquo;s not too far outside my comfort zone. It&rsquo;s still tech. It&rsquo;s still coding. But it <em>feels</em> like a different world.</p>

<p>It&rsquo;s also a return to the math and physics of my engineering days, and to coding with C++, which has changed significantly in the 15 years I&rsquo;ve been away from the language. There&rsquo;s metric ton of learning to do. The goal is to distill this learning down to a collection of college level game development courses.</p>

<p><a href="https://www.notion.so/Kyle-is-Learning-in-Public-ca7e12c5569d49029fc8f1059a8392be">I&rsquo;ll be documenting my learning here</a>. Join me.</p>
