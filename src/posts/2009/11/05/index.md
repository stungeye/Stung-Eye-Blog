## A Catch

<p>How’s life? I’m reading <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catch-22">Catch-22</a>. I laugh out loud on the bus. You know? Real life lols. It’s a little dated, but luckily I know who <a href="http://images.google.ca/images?q=henry+fonda">Henry Fonda</a> is.</p>

<p><em>“You’re inches away from death every time you go on a mission. How much older can you be at your age?”</em></p>

<p>The <a href="http://copyright.econsultation.ca/">Canadian Copyright Consultation</a> ended in September. The <a href="http://www.ic.gc.ca/eic/site/008.nsf/eng/02870.html">submission from the Canadian Music Creators Coalition</a> (<a href="http://www.musiccreators.ca/wp/">CMCC</a>) is quite enlightened, the <a href="http://www.ic.gc.ca/eic/site/008.nsf/eng/02823.html">submission from Don Hogarth</a> of the <a href="http://www.cria.ca/">CRIA</a> (Canadian Recording Industry Association of America) is not.</p>

<p>Meanwhile, Canadian officials are taking part in negotiations concerning a top-secret copyright treaty called the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anti-Counterfeiting_Trade_Agreement">Anti-Counterfeiting Trade Agreement</a> that could see families barred from the Internet for a year if someone in the household is <em>suspected</em> of illegal downloads. [<a href="http://www.calgaryherald.com/business/Canada+talks+over+copyright+laws+with+bite/2189494/story.html">source</a>]</p>

<p>Even though copyright was created to protect the commercial interests of content creators, the ACTA includes provisions for the <strong>criminal</strong> enforcement of “willful copyright and trademark infringement even where there is no direct or indirect motivation of financial gain.” [<a href="http://www.boingboing.net/2009/11/04/more-on-secret-copyr.html">source</a>]</p>

<p>Might I one day be jailed for making mixtapes or <a href="http://www.divshare.com/download/142987-afa">posting my mixes to the net</a>?</p>

<p>Listen, I’ve said it before, I’m not a pirate I just grog a lot.</p>
