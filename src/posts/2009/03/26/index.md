[The Free Will Theorem](http://www.princeton.edu/main/news/archive/S23/75/58A30/index.xml?section=featured)

<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Horton_Conway">John Conway</a> (of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conway%27s_Game_of_Life">Game of Life</a> fame) is currently presenting a series of public lectures at Princeton to explain his work with <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ax%E2%80%93Kochen_theorem">Simon Kochen</a> on the free will of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elementary_particle">elementary particles</a>.</p>

<p>They assert, roughly, <em>&#8220;that if indeed we humans have free will, then elementary particles already have their own small share of this valuable commodity. [&#8230; A] particle’s response [to human intervention] (to be pedantic—the universe’s response near the particle) is not determined by the entire previous history of the universe.&#8221;</em></p>

<p>For the Mathematicians and Physicists: <a href="http://arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/0604079">The Free Will Theorem</a></p>
