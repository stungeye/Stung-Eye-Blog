[Glasnost: Test if Your ISP is Manipulating BitTorrent Traffic](http://broadband.mpi-sws.org/transparency/bttest-mlab.php)

<p>The <a href="http://www.cbc.ca">Canadian Broadcasting Corporation</a> (CBC) is required by <a href="http://www.cbc.radio-canada.ca/about/mandate.shtml">mandate</a> to make its programming &#8220;available throughout Canada by the most appropriate and efficient means.&#8221; Using <a href="http://www.bittorrent.com/">BitTorrent</a> allows the CBC to meet this target. However, when <a href="http://www.cbc.ca/technology/story/2008/03/26/bittorrent-cbc.html">they experimented with this technology</a> last year, many users were blocked by their ISP due to <a href="http://piratebay.org">BitTorrent&#8217;s association with piracy</a>.</p>

<p>The <a href="http://broadband.mpi-sws.org/transparency/results/">Glosnost Results</a> show that at least 10 Canadian ISPs block BitTorrent outright. <a href="http://www.techdirt.com/articles/20080514/1748391117.shtml">Canadian ISPs are also starting use traffic-shaping techniques</a> to throttle-down the speed of all BitTorrent traffic.</p>

<p>Discrimination ain&#8217;t nice in meat-space, and it ain&#8217;t nice on the net.  If you love the internet, read up on <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Network_neutrality">Network Neutrality</a>. Non-discriminatory treatment of network-traffic is essential for an open and participatory internet.</p>
