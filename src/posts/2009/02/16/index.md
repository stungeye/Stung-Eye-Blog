[New Theories of Mimesis](http://ask.metafilter.com/114323/New-theories-of-Mimesis-in-digitalhypertextualhypermedial-cultures)

<p><em>&#8220;I am looking for writings on <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mimesis">Mimesis</a> in regards new, digital, hypertext and hypermedial technologies and cultures.</em></p>

<p><em>I am following the redefinition of mimesis. From Plato&#8217;s disregard of oral culture, through his mimesis of Socrates&#8217; dialogues in writing. Following Plato, Aristotle&#8217;s theory was always a written mimesis, thus the order and processes of representation and mimicry were fundamentally written.</em></p>

<p><em>In essence, I am interested in how the artifacts of oral culture differed in their mimesis to written culture, and thus, how our modern move from a written to a digital/hypertextual culture will similarly impact on mimetic embodiment.</em>&#8221;</p>
