<p>The world is mapped using a grid of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Longitude">longitudes</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latitude">latitudes</a>. There is a degree confluence point where each line of longitude crosses each line of latitude.</p>

<p>On the weekend Jody, Mike and I hiked into the woods towards <a href="http://maps.google.com/maps?f=q&amp;hl=en&amp;geocode=&amp;q=52,-99&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;t=h&amp;ll=51.835778,-98.585815&amp;spn=2.294657,4.938354&amp;z=8">an unvisited degree confluence</a> here in Manitoba&#8217;s interlake region (just north of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gypsumville,_Manitoba">Gypsumville</a>).</p>

<p>We brought a GPS and some maps. We were given advice and direction by the local Conservation Officer. Our packs were lined with garbage bags.</p>

<p>One the first day we crossed <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/stungeye/3723488161/in/set-72157621509390694/">a swamp</a> by convincing ourselves to walk in deeper and deeper water. Eventually we swam, but only for a few doggy paddles.</p>

<p>On the second day we crossed swamps by <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/stungeye/3723525735/in/set-72157621509390694/">sloshing atop floating mats of tangled reeds</a>.</p>

<p>See: <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/stungeye/sets/72157621509390694/">Our Confluence Scouting Photos</a> or <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/stungeye/sets/72157621509390694/show/">Slideshow</a></p>

<p>Another attempt will be required to reach the point.</p>
